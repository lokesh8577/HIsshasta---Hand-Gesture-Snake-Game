Snake Security Policy

We take the security and integrity of Snake Game very seriously. If you have found a vulnerability, please report it ASAP so we can quickly remediate the issue.

Table of Contents

  • How to Disclose a Vulnerability
  • Submission Guidelines

How to Disclose a Vulnerability

For vulnerabilities that impact the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Snake Game services, please send your disclosure via email.

For non-security related platform bugs, follow the bug submission guidelines.

Include as much detail as possible to ensure reproducibility. At a minimum, vulnerability disclosures should include:

  • Vulnerability Description
  • Proof of Concept
  • Impact Assessment
  • Screenshots or Proof
  • Steps to Reproduce
  • Suggested Fix (if possible)

Submission Guidelines

Do not engage in activities that might cause a denial of service condition, create significant strains on critical resources, or negatively impact users of the site outside of test accounts.

Allowed Testing

You may test for vulnerabilities on your own accounts or test accounts you create. Testing should not:

  • Violate any laws or regulations
  • Disrupt or degrade our services
  • Access or modify data without authorization
  • Use automated scanning tools that generate significant traffic
  • Perform social engineering or phishing against our staff or users

What We Promise

When working with security researchers acting in good faith, we will:

  • Acknowledge receipt of your report within 3 business days
  • Provide regular updates on our progress in addressing the vulnerability
  • Work to remediate valid vulnerabilities in a timely manner
  • Recognize your contribution if you are the first to report a unique vulnerability (unless you prefer to remain anonymous)

Safe Harbor

We consider security research conducted in accordance with this policy to be:

  • Authorized in view of any applicable anti-hacking laws
  • Exempt from our Terms of Service restrictions
  • Lawful, helpful to the overall security of the internet, and conducted in good faith
